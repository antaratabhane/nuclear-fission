# ⚛️ Nuclear Fission Visualization

This project presents a simple and interactive visualization of the **nuclear fission process** using web technologies. The objective of this project is to demonstrate how a heavy atomic nucleus splits into smaller nuclei after absorbing a neutron, releasing energy and additional neutrons that may initiate a chain reaction. The visualization focuses on the well-known fission reaction of Uranium-235 and aims to help viewers understand the structure and outcome of the reaction in a clear and intuitive way.

## 🧪 Concept of Nuclear Fission

Nuclear fission is a fundamental nuclear reaction in which a **heavy atomic nucleus becomes unstable and divides into two lighter nuclei** after capturing a neutron. During this process, a considerable amount of energy is released along with additional neutrons. These emitted neutrons can further interact with other nuclei, creating a **chain reaction** that continues the fission process.

A commonly represented reaction is:

U-235 + n → Ba-144 + Kr-89 + 3n + Energy

This reaction demonstrates how a small amount of mass is converted into a large quantity of energy according to the principles of nuclear physics.

## 🎯 Purpose of the Project

The primary objective of this project is to provide a **clear and simple visual representation of a nuclear fission reaction** using basic front-end technologies. The project acts as a small educational demonstration designed to help students, developers, and science enthusiasts understand how the fission reaction is structured and how the products are formed.

It also highlights how scientific ideas can be communicated effectively through simple web-based visualizations without relying on complex simulations.
